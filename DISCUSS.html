→ READING DISCUSSION I

In The Programmed Designer and The Internet's Back to the Land Movement readings, I've come to understand that in terms of design and technologies, the act of commodifying for profit is often evident; especially from corporations. This act changes the intent or purpose of what design and technology initially offers for the individual and is what the two readings articulates. Design and technologies are two areas that integrate the idea of the Utopian world, and one that utilizes self-expression. Commodifying those two areas engenders a standard that limits creativity, growth, and independence which corporations establish that upsets the works of independent artists and technological communities.

What the two readings then suggest is for those independent artists and communities to seek different ways to escape from corporate practices by looking at different mediums and/or programs for artists, and for tech communities to engage in a more peer-to-peer community. As an example, I try to integrate relief printing into my designs as it helps me to study letterforms and it allows me to create organic forms that are not too restrictive. For technology, I push myself to take a break when I become too invested to the trends or style of certain design works. These techniques do help sometimes, but avoiding corporate standards is difficult but not impossible.

→ READING DISCUSSION II

Universal Users can also be known as the General Purpose User, one that is not limited to the devices and applications in which they create in. In this article by Olia Lialina, the Universal User can be viewed as an individual who seeks out their aspirations regardless of the mediums or tools one uses to achieve it, one who liaise with hardware and software. An example Lialina uses is how an individual can use alternative applications for the same features of another application - using a website instead of Twitter to blog. In regards to Schwulst’s argument, individual websites provide the independence in which Universal Users are creating for themselves on other platforms that restrict accessibility. Lialina demonstrates this notion as everyday working individuals creating work on programs that were intended for different purposes, but still producing what was expected; using “Live Journal” as Twitter and using Twitter as Twitter.

In reference to Olia Lialina’s concepts of computer users, in some aspects I would describe myself as a General Purpose User given that I like to discover various ways of completing tasks at a sufficient pace. I often find myself using different applications intended for different purposes solely because it is simple to use and produces the same outcomes as it would with the appropriate applications. For example, using phone applications and producing work from there, rather than using inDesign just to push out more personal works.

→ READING DISCUSSION IV

ito Steyerl's point that linear perspective privileges the individual is referring to the notion that the visual paradigm has changed to a perspective that sought for dominance. On one end of the paradigm is an individual's personal gains from the inconvenience of another on the other end of this paradigm. Steyerl demonstrates this understanding with a painting that displays the downfall of the Jews and Muslims in Spain. A woman sells a host to a Jewish merchant who tries to desecrate it, only to end up at stake along with his wife and two children which led to the expulsion of his people in Spain. That is linear perspective, where the conditions are passed on down the figurative line rather than it looping back to the initial point and refers to the paradigm of privileging one but also lessening one's humanity. Hence the scientific worldview converting into a set standard for making people as other and gaining dominance over them.

As ambitious as it is to strive to maintain both a humanistic and technological perspective of the paradigm, one will always dominate over the other much like the contemporary views of the linear perspective. Candidly, I believe a humanistic approach would always dominate over a technical perspective as it is in our nature to emphasize personal worth and fulfill one's potential. However, it depends on how one approaches the measures for personal worth without having to lower another. Aside from that, taking on a humanistic perspective enables one to understand one another and perceive one another as humans that need to be treated fairly. Essentially, one can't be too technical to understand the complex behaviour of humans.
